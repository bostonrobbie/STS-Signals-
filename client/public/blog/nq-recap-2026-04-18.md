### Notable setup of the week

The Thursday 10:15 AM Trend (T3) long. Here's why it matters:

Price had been pinned in a tight 80-point range for the first 45 minutes after the open. CPI data from the day before was still driving positioning. Our T3 variant looks for a clear break of the 30-minute high with volume confirmation AND price holding above the morning VWAP.

At 10:15 exactly, price broke 22,340 on elevated volume. The T3 signal fired: long entry 22,355, stop 22,295 (-60 pts), target 22,420 (+65 pts). Within 45 minutes, price had pushed through target and the bracket order exited automatically at 22,402. +$1,900 per Mini contract, +$190 per Micro.

The reason I highlight this one: **a discretionary trader would have missed it.** The move looked "choppy" in the 30 seconds before the breakout — the kind of false-start pattern that makes experienced discretionary traders wait for "confirmation." By the time a discretionary trader had confirmation, the move was half-over. The systematic rules don't hesitate on ambiguity — when the conditions are met, the signal fires.

## What didn't work

Wednesday's L-ORB long. CPI was 60 minutes away, the morning range set up with a reasonable breakout level, the signal fired at 9:45 ET long at 22,180. Price stalled almost immediately, drifted back through the entry, and stopped at 22,127 for a -$1,060 Mini loss.

This is a known weakness of any systematic breakout strategy: **pre-data-release periods have lower signal quality.** A discretionary trader would have skipped this trade because "CPI is 90 minutes away and there's no directional bias." The systematic strategy doesn't make that judgment — it takes the setup and absorbs the occasional pre-event stop-out. Over the 15-year backtest, these pre-event losses are part of what gives us the confidence that the rules aren't optimized for any particular regime.
